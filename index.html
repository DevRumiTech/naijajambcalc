How JAMB Aggregate Scores Work

Nigerian institutions often combine your UTME result with your O’level performance to create an aggregate score. There is no single “official” formula used by all schools, so this tool uses a simple, transparent method to help you estimate your standing.

  • UTME (0–400) is normalized to 0–100 by dividing by 4.
  • O’level grades are converted to points (A1 highest, C6 lowest in this tool) and averaged.
  • A weighting split (50/50, 60/40, or 70/30) combines both into one estimated percentage.

Important: This is an estimate only. Schools may add post-UTME, departmental rules, subject requirements, or different conversions. Use this as a planning guide, not a guarantee.

Common Mistakes Students Make

  • Assuming all schools calculate the same way. Each institution can use its own method.
  • Ignoring O’level impact. Strong grades can significantly improve your combined estimate.
  • Using rumours as cutoffs. Always verify requirements from official school sources.
  • Entering incomplete grades. If you select fewer than 5 subjects, this tool averages what you provide.

Tip: Use the weighting selector to see different “what-if” outcomes, then focus your effort where it helps most (UTME performance, stronger O’level grades, or both).

FAQ (Parents & Students)

Is this an official JAMB calculator?

No. NaijaJAMBCalc is an independent tool. It provides a simplified estimate and is not affiliated with JAMB.

Can this calculator predict admission?

No. Admission depends on your chosen school and course, competition, requirements, and any screening steps. This tool is best used for guidance and planning.

Why is my score different from someone else’s?

Your estimate changes based on UTME score, selected grades, and weighting. Even small grade differences can affect the O’level average.

Are all universities the same?

No. Schools can apply different formulas or additional criteria (screening, post-UTME, subject requirements). Always check your institution’s official information.

What if I don’t know my final grades yet?

Enter your most likely grades to run a quick estimate. When your final result is available, update the grades to see your new estimate instantly.
